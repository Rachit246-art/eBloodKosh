Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Who can donate blood?

Anyone between 18-65 years of age, in good health, and meeting the basic eligibility criteria can donate blood.

2. How often can I donate blood?

Whole blood can typically be donated every 3 months. Platelet and plasma donations may have different intervals.

3. Is blood donation safe?

Yes. Blood donation is safe and sterile. All equipment is single-use and discarded after donation.

4. What should I do before donating blood?

Eat a healthy meal, stay hydrated, and avoid alcohol before donation. Bring a valid ID with you.

5. How is donated blood used?

Donated blood is used in emergencies, surgeries, cancer treatment, chronic illness management, and more.
